Full Interview Process

From CV submission to final hire — review the step-by-step flow and the key points at each stage.

Step by Step

The 7-step journey
to your hire

1
CV Submission
Your first impression starts with the CV.
Preparation Points
• Work Experience is the core — describe service-related experience in concrete detail
 • Service work experience is the core
 • Avoid gaps of more than 2 months between roles
 • No gaps of 2+ months between roles
 • Photo: bright smile, clean background, professional attire
2
Video Interview & Small Talk
Held as an online video interview or as Open Day CV small talk.
Preparation Points
• Giving a great impression is everything — this stage is essentially an image interview
 • Giving a great impression is the core
 • You don't need to speak at length, but practise responding to questions instantly
 • Practise responding to questions instantly
 • Practise smiling while you speak — never forget the smile
 • Same goes for small talk — keep a natural, warm smile throughout • Same goes for small talk
3
1st Presentation
Held in group or individual presentation format.
Preparation Points
• Individual presentation is the main format — the key is how you deliver a given topic in front of the crowd
 • Individual presentation is the main format
 • With many candidates, brevity is a virtue — keep only the essence, cleanly delivered
• The essence of presenting in front of a crowd is attitude — confident voice and bright expression are key
4
2nd Discussion
A specific scenario and customer list are given.
Preparation Points
• Both the topic and the customer list are given in concrete detail
• Every person on the list is our customer — think first from the interviewer's perspective
 • Every person on the list is our customer — think interviewer-first
 • Listening to and empathising with other candidates while actively participating matters greatly • Listen, empathise, and actively participate
5
English Test
30-question multiple-choice fill-in-the-blank.
Preparation Points
• Vocabulary, prepositions, and modal verbs in cabin / safety / service contexts
• e.g. "must not smoke on board", "under maintenance", "prior to entering"
• Build everyday English reading + practical service expressions — pair with daily English shadowing
6
Roleplay
Real-world service situation responses.
Preparation Points
• Often runs as a continuation of the 2nd Discussion topic — not required every round, but must be prepared for
 • Often follows the 2nd Discussion topic — be ready
 • Real cabin / hotel / restaurant service scenarios are given
 • Real cabin / hotel / restaurant scenarios
 • Apology → empathy → solution → alternative → follow-up, all flowing naturally
• Acknowledge the customer's feelings first, then present the solution in concrete detail
7
Final Interview
1:1 final interview — the last gate.
Preparation Points
• You must showcase the qualities of a cabin crew through your own answers, grounded in your experience
 • Showcase cabin crew qualities through your own answers
 • Don't try to think of episodes on the spot — prepare your best episodes in advance
 • Prepare your best episodes in advance
 • This is the final gate — prepare it thoroughly • Final gate — go strong
