The Open Day Group Discussion is where international airline interviewers assess "can this person actually collaborate with other crew on a 12-hour flight?" Speaking timing and respect for others matter more than English fluency.

Speaking timing that gets hired

  • Being the 2nd or 3rd speaker is safest — the first carries the pressure, the last is forgotten
  • Quote one specific point another candidate just made → add your own view
  • Take roughly your fair share of total airtime (not too much, not too little)

A hire-ready phrase template

"I really like what Sarah said about teamwork. To add to that, I think communication is also key — especially when working with passengers from different cultures."

Bonus points for the summariser

Whoever wraps up with a natural "So if I summarise what we've discussed…" stands out to the interviewer — leadership and listening, demonstrated together.

Frequent discussion topics

  • "What is the most important quality of a cabin crew?"
  • "Design your ideal in-flight service"
  • "Solve this customer complaint scenario"
  • "Choose 3 items to bring on a desert island"
