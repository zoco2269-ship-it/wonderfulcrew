Round 1 Discussion

Present confidently in front of the camera.
It is not What you say — it's How you say it.

Key points

  • Why a presentation: The interviewer is checking whether you can stay calm in front of hundreds of passengers in an emergency.
  • Keep it to 4–5 clean sentences. Use simple words. Reaching for a hard word and stumbling costs points.
  • Smile + confidence + warm tone. Bright, warm energy is what passes this round.

Topic 1

Self-introduction including service experience + your favorite travel destination.

Topic 2

Self-introduction including service experience + the celebrity you'd want to sit next to on a flight.

Topic 3

Name, age, customer service experience + the food you want to try after joining Emirates.

Round 1 of an Open Day is an image interview. The interviewer is not grading the cleverness of your answer — they're checking whether you can stand in front of strangers and stay composed. Cabin crew work begins with safety briefings to a packed cabin, so this round filters out the candidates who freeze.

What actually wins Round 1

  • Smile from the first second. A warm face beats a clever sentence.
  • 4–5 short sentences max. Going longer makes you sound unsure.
  • Easy vocabulary you trust. A stumble on a fancy word costs more than the word earns.

A clean Round 1 template

"Hi, my name is [Name]. I worked at [place] for two years where I learned to enjoy serving people from many backgrounds. My favorite destination is Bali — I love the warm hospitality there. Thank you."
