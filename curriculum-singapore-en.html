Singapore Airlines Curriculum

Singapore Airlines
Interview Preparation

Based in Singapore. Prepare for the SIA interview — renowned for the world's highest standard of cabin service.

Online Application→ Video Interview→ Assessment Day→ Final Interview

Stage 1: Online Application

CV · Photos · Eligibility
  • Apply online via the Singapore Airlines Careers portal
  • Upload your English CV
  • Minimum height: 158 cm (female) / 165 cm (male)
  • Education: degree-equivalent (GCE O Level or above)
TIP: The "Singapore Girl / Singapore Boy" image is iconic. A poised, elegant first impression is essential.

Stage 2: Online Video Interview

Self-Recorded · Scenario Questions · Written Essay
  • Self-recorded video interview (HireVue platform)
  • Scenario-based responses, English essay, and full-length self-recording included
Sample Singapore Airlines Video Interview Questions (4 items)
  1. Tell me about the smartest person you know personally and why.
  2. What would you do if you saw another cabin crew being rude to a passenger?
  3. Essay: Tell me about a time you had set yourself a difficult goal. (10 mins, 400 words)
  4. Show the full length of yourself and give a big smile after you're done.
TIP: Choose a clean background and front-facing light. The essay is a time-management exercise — 2 mins to outline, 7 mins to write, 1 min to proof. For the full-length recording, hold a natural smile from start to finish.

Stage 3: Assessment Day

Debate · Kebaya Fitting · Skin Check
  • Debate — Groups of nine split into three sub-groups. After a topic and side (for/against) are assigned, you have 3 minutes to prepare. Each candidate then stands up and delivers their position once — it's not a back-and-forth debate but a one-way presentation.
    e.g. "Money can buy happiness" / "Should men make decisions?" — Confidence and clear delivery matter more than perfect fluency. Many candidates have passed.
  • Oral English assessment
  • Kebaya Fitting + Skin Check (Grooming)
    · Kebaya is worn with sandals (stockings removed); a tidy pedicure is recommended
    · Skin check examines face, décolleté and arms-raised back skin under daylight
    · Walking is repeated while smiling — body proportion, balance, hair, and makeup are carefully assessed
TIP: The panel is typically two interviewers — an HR representative from Singapore HQ and either a Korean or Singaporean assessor. During the kebaya fitting and skin check, posture, expression and confidence in uniform are key. Every moment of Assessment Day is being evaluated — maintain your composure even while waiting.

Stage 4: Final Interview

2:1 Panel · HQ Representative + Local Office Interviewer
  • 2:1 panel (a male HQ representative + a female local-office interviewer)
  • Tell me about yourself / Why did you resign?
  • Why didn't you go back to your previous airline? / Why SIA?
  • What would you do if your senior is younger than you?
  • Follow-up questions on service, overseas experience, adaptability and English study
  • Possibility of a roleplay segment
TIP: Consistency between expression and tone is crucial. When asked about resigning, answer honestly and with sincerity — no embellishment. Weave organizational adaptability and teamwork into your answers. Maintain eye contact and a composed expression throughout.
