Singapore Airlines Curriculum

Singapore Airlines
Complete Interview Prep

Singapore-based. Renowned for world-class service. Prepare for the Singapore Airlines cabin crew interview, end to end.

Online Application→ Video Interview→ Assessment Day→ Final Interview

Stage 1: Online Application

Resume · Photo · Eligibility
  • Apply online via the Singapore Airlines careers site
  • Upload an English-language resume
  • Minimum height: women 158cm / men 165cm
  • Education: bachelor's degree or above (GCE O Level minimum)
TIP: Singapore Airlines is built around the "Singapore Girl/Boy" image. A poised, elegant impression matters above all.

Stage 2: Online Video Interview

Self-recorded · scenario questions · essay
  • Self-recorded video interview (HireVue platform)
  • Scenario-based answers + English essay + full-body shot
4 actual SIA video interview questions
  1. Tell me about the smartest person you know personally and why.
  2. What would you do if you saw another cabin crew being rude to a passenger?
  3. Essay: Tell me about a time you had set yourself a difficult goal. (10 mins, 400 words)
  4. Show the full length of yourself and give a big smile after you're done.
TIP: Keep the background clean and the lighting flat on your face. The essay is all about time management — 2 min ideation + 7 min writing + 1 min review. For the full-body shot, hold a natural smile right through to the end.

Stage 3: Assessment Day

Debate · kebaya fitting · scar check
  • Debate — groups of 9, splitting into smaller groups of 3. Once a topic and side (for/against) are given, you have 3 minutes to organise your thoughts, then each candidate stands and delivers their position once. It's not a back-and-forth debate but a one-way presentation format.
    e.g. "Money can buy happiness" / "Should men make decisions?" — what counts is confident, articulate delivery, not perfect fluency. Many alumni have passed this stage.
  • English oral assessment
  • Kebaya fitting + Scar check (grooming)
    · When wearing the kebaya, remove stockings and switch to sandals; a clean pedicure is recommended
    · Scar check: under daylight they inspect facial skin, the chest below the collarbone, and the back when arms are raised
    · Walk repeatedly while smiling — body proportions, balance, and hair/makeup are all examined closely
TIP: Usually 2 interviewers — an HR officer from Singapore HQ + a Korean or Singaporean panellist. The kebaya fitting and scar check evaluate posture, expression and confidence in uniform. Every moment of Assessment Day is being assessed, so keep your posture, expression and walk on point even while waiting.

Stage 4: Final Interview

2:1 interview · HQ HR + regional panellist
  • 2:1 interview (HQ male HR officer + regional female panellist)
  • Tell me about yourself / Why did you resign?
  • Why didn't you go back to your previous airline? / Why SIA?
  • What would you do if your senior is younger than you?
  • Follow-ups on service experience, overseas experience, adaptability, English study
  • Roleplay possible
TIP: Consistency between expression and tone is key. When asked about resignation reasons, answer honestly without varnish — show real sincerity. Weave organisational fit and teamwork into your answers, and maintain eye contact and composed expression to come across as poised.
