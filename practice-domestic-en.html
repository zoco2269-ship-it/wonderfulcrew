✨ WonderfulCrew's Korean Domestic Airline Master Tips ▼ Expand to read

Korean Air, Asiana, Jeju Air, Jin Air, T'way, Air Busan, Air Premia and other Korean domestic carriers run a different interview from international airlines. Interviews are conducted in Korean and evaluated stage by stage. WonderfulCrew has 13 years of data from successful applicants.

The 4 Stages of the Korean Domestic Interview

  • Stage 1 — Video Interview — 30s-1min self-introduction and motivation. Camera presence, grooming, steady delivery.
  • Stage 2 — 1st Panel Interview — Group of 8 in front of working-level interviewers. Common questions plus individual questions. Listening manners while others answer.
  • Stage 3 — Executive Interview — Personality and service-mindset evaluation. STAR method is effective.
  • Stage 4 — English Test + Fitness + Medical — Asiana requires a 25m swim. The English portion focuses on natural conversation.

Self-Introduction Tips by Airline

  • Korean Air: "Excellence in Flight" value tied to your own experience. Structure your 1-minute video answer.
  • Asiana: "Beautiful People" — kindness, passion, maturity, challenge, effort. Use concrete examples.
  • Jeju Air: "New Path" — emphasize LCC multitasking and practicality.
  • Jin Air: Korean Air affiliate plus an approachable image. Be clear on "Why Jin Air and not Korean Air?"
  • T'way: "Together as One" teamwork. Spirit of challenge and growth story.
  • Air Busan / Air Seoul: Local-base residency preferred. Japanese / Chinese language proficiency is a plus.

Common Korean Domestic Interview Questions

  • "Why our airline?"
  • "What does service mean to you?"
  • "Your hardest moment and how you overcame it."
  • "Leadership experience from your school years."
  • "A passenger has spilled a drink — how would you respond?"
  • "How do you handle a disagreement with a fellow crew?"
  • "3-minute self-promotion" / "1-minute self-introduction"

How Successful Applicants Prepare

  • 30 min daily refining self-introduction and motivation (record and review).
  • Weekly 1:1 coach video feedback (twice a week).
  • Mock interview study with applicants for the same airline in the community.
  • Full grooming setup (hair bun + suit + makeup) practiced in advance.
  • Asiana applicants: 25m swim training in advance (2-3 sessions/week for 2 months).
