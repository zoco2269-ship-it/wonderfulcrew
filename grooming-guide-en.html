Image Making & Grooming

Cabin Crew Image Making
& Grooming Mastery

At an airline interview, your first impression is half the offer. Master the grooming standards expected by international carriers and walk in fully prepared.

International Airlines

International Airline Grooming Guide

Important: International airlines—especially Middle Eastern carriers—prefer makeup that may feel bolder than what you are used to. A soft, barely-there look can read as "no makeup at all" to an international interviewer, so apply it noticeably more defined than your everyday style.

💄 Makeup

International carriers, particularly Middle Eastern airlines such as Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Etihad, expect a polished, clearly defined look. If you find yourself wondering "is this a touch too bold?"—that is usually the right level.

  • 🎨 Base: Full-coverage foundation for a flawless, smooth complexion
  • 👁 Eyes: Well-defined eyeshadow in brown, gold, or burgundy tones with a clear, bold eyeliner
  • Lashes: False lashes are strongly recommended—choose full, lengthening styles
  • 💋 Lips: Red or deep coral. A vivid lip colour is the standard
  • 🖌️ Contouring: Shade along the nose bridge and jawline for dimension—a sculpted face is essential
  • Highlighter: A subtle glow on the cheekbones and nose bridge

Everyday Makeup

  • Natural semi-matte finish
  • Soft brown eyeshadow
  • MLBB lip colour
  • False lashes optional
  • Minimal contouring

International Airline Makeup

  • Full coverage, flawless skin
  • Defined eyeshadow and eyeliner
  • Red or deep-coral statement lip
  • False lashes strongly recommended
  • Contouring and highlighter essential

💇 Hair

  • Always an updo: A clean chignon or low bun with absolutely no flyaways—hairspray is non-negotiable
  • Hairnet: A fine hairnet placed over the bun creates an even cleaner finish
  • Colour: Natural tones are the safest choice. Avoid overly bright blonde shades
  • Baby hairs: Smooth down the hairline using a fine-tooth comb and strong-hold spray for a polished finish

👔 Attire

  • Style: Business formal. A dress, skirt suit, or trouser suit with a tailored jacket all work
  • Colour: Choose a shade that flatters you. You do not need to mirror Emirates red or Qatar burgundy
  • Note: Avoid mini skirts and overly tight silhouettes. Choose fabrics with stretch in case of an arm-reach test
  • 👠 Shoes: Classic pumps in a dark tone, 5–7 cm heel
  • 🧦 Hosiery: Nude tights. Laddered tights are a clear no—always carry spares

💅 Nails & Accessories

  • 💅 Nails: Always polished—clear varnish is acceptable. It signals that you are service-ready
  • Accessories: Small pearl or cubic earrings. Nothing oversized or attention-seeking
  • ⌚ Watch: Wear one—it symbolises punctuality and reliability
  • 🌺 Fragrance: Light and subtle only. Middle Eastern carriers fly fragrance-sensitive passengers, so restraint matters

📏 Scars / Tattoos / Height / Arm Reach

  • Scars: Emirates tends to be more lenient than Qatar Airways. Cover small marks with concealer; larger scars must be declared
  • Tattoos: Any tattoo visible in uniform (arms, wrists, hands, ankles) is disqualifying. Tattoos in hidden areas are usually fine when properly declared
  • Height: Emirates requires roughly 160 cm. Daily yoga and stretching can add a small but real margin
  • Arm reach: 212 cm to close overhead bins. Going on tiptoe is permitted. Keep up consistent shoulder mobility work

Interview-Day Essentials

  • Multiple printed copies of your CV
  • Original passport
  • Spare passport-size photos
  • Full-length photograph (for airlines that request one)
  • Spare tights (at least two pairs)
  • Full makeup touch-up kit
  • Spare false lashes and adhesive
  • Hairspray, pins, and hairnet
  • Concealer (for scar coverage)
  • Clear nail varnish (for quick chip repairs)
  • Water and mints
  • Comfortable walking shoes plus your interview heels
